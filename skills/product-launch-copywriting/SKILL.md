# Product Launch Copywriting

## Narrative

- Begin with the audience's desire, tension, identity, or outcome.
- Reveal the product early. Let its action establish meaning.
- Shape the middle as one progression: first value -> expansion -> confidence -> completion.
- Introduce control, trust, and implementation depth after value is felt.
- Return to the audience at the close. Let the ending answer or transform the opening.
- Give each beat one narrative purpose.

## Language

- Use short, forceful clauses and low sentence density.
- Leave room for the product and the medium to finish the thought.
- Let the copy carry the promise and emotional movement. Let product evidence carry secondary facts.
- Prioritize rhythm, compression, emotional clarity, and memorability.
- Allow metaphor, ellipsis, semantic shifts, and deliberate ambiguity when the central meaning holds.
- Preserve approved lines verbatim.

## Judgment

- Center one audience and one message.
- Keep the central promise true. Omit qualifications that do not change it.
- Select product details that prove the promise or advance the progression.
- Prefer fewer, stronger beats to exhaustive coverage.
- Keep sentence density low without fragmenting the narrative.
- End with human reassurance or possibility, then give the brand space.
